Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Looking at the scatter plot, we can generally conclude that there is a positive relationship between the percentage of lacking healthcare and the percentage in poverty of a state. That is, with the percentage of poverty increases, lack of healthcare also increases.

Notably Texas has highest rate of lacking healthcare, but its poverty rate is not the highest. Also worth to mention that Massachusetts has lowest rate of lacking healthcare among all states, even through it is not the richest states.
